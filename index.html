Should Pluto be a planet again?

The Story of Pluto

Pluto was discovered in 1930 by astronomer Clyde Tombaugh. For 76 years, it was considered the 9th planet in our Solar System.

However, in 2006, the International Astronomical Union (IAU) held a meeting and made a historic decision. They created a new definition for what counts as a planet. To be a planet, a body must:

  • Orbit the Sun
  • Have enough gravity to be roughly round
  • Clear the neighborhood around its orbit

Pluto failed the third condition because it shares its orbit with many other objects in the Kuiper Belt. So it was reclassified as a "Dwarf Planet".

Many scientists and people around the world were upset by this decision, and the debate continues to this day!
